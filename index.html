Washington, D.C., Nov. 23, 2024 — Whytni Kernodle

Black women are at the epicenter of America’s wage inequality crisis, according to a 2024 report by the National Women’s Law Center.

Earning just 67 cents for every dollar paid to white men. Black women face a lifetime wage gap of up to $2.4 million USD. This disparity worsens for those with advanced degrees, underscoring systemic barriers that no level of education can erase.

Despite being overrepresented in essential roles like healthcare and education, Black women are still funneled into low-paying jobs. "It’s a vicious cycle," says finacial analysis Elle Theodore. "We can’t build wealth if we’re constantly undervalued."

The data highlights an uncomfortable truth: Black women, who are often the backbone of their families and communities, must navigate a system that undervalues their labor and sacrifices.

Black women shoulder not only wage gaps but also disproportionate caregiving and economic instability, according to Theodore. "This isn’t just about dollars lost—it’s about a legacy of missed opportunities, stolen potential, and the question of how a nation can thrive while sidelining those who contribute so much to its success.
